I am an associate political scientist at the RAND Corporation. Prior to joining RAND, I was a postdoctoral fellow at the Institute for Public Policy and Social Research.

I earned my PhD in political science at Michigan State University. My dissertation, which involved both fieldwork in Lebanon and large-scale data analysis, explored how armed non-state groups strategically use violence and media to influence the public. My research is published or accepted at the Journal of Politics, Political Behavior, International Interactions, and Civil Wars.
